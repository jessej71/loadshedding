Data driven investigating of an energy crisis in South Africa

South African state run electricity provider Eskom cuts power to predetermined locations when demand exceeds supply. This is called load shedding and has led to a energy crisis. In Figure 1, the impact for the last decade is shown in terms of the the monthly average of daily power cuts experienced by South Africans.

Data source: sepush.co.za.

Some suggest severe load shedding is explained by extreme precipitation events. The hypothesis would be that because much of South Africa's energy is produced by coal that can become water saturated and not burn well, heavy rains lower energy production. To test this, Figure 2 plots power interuptions vs precipitation.
